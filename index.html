1935 WPA NPS Inspiration Map

Cecilia Vanden Heuvel

This slippy map took inspiration from the 1935 Workers Progress Administration (WPA) National Park Service (NPS) Posters. Specifically, the color palette was taken from the North Cascades National Park poster. Elements of rich, saturated and contrasting colors can be seen in the slippy map. The National Parks and other public lands are emphasized to reiterate the original inspiration.

These images represent the design board for this map. I found images that reflected a similar color palette and minimalist style.

Here is the full specification sheet for this map.

This set of images demonstrate how the map features shift with zoom. In this example, Yosemite National Park and the surrounding area is highlighted. With the addition of NPS trail routes, the viewer has a heightened understanding of the National Parks and their space.
